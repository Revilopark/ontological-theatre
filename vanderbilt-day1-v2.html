Chapter 1 · Key Concept Network Topology
The Three Shapes That Determine Power

The physical layer shapes everything above it.

🌲 Tree Topology
Hierarchical. Top node controls all flow. Efficient for command-and-control. Fragile — remove root, whole network fails.
e.g. AT&T pre-1984, org charts, broadcast TV
🔗 Mesh Topology
Distributed. Every node connects to many others. Resilient — no single point of failure. Power distributes.
e.g. The internet, VOIP, peer-to-peer networks
⭐ Small-World / Scale-Free
High-degree hubs dominate. Power-law distribution. Most nodes have few edges; a few have thousands.
e.g. Twitter, Instagram influencers, VC networks

Chapter 2 The Social Organism
The Framework, Not The Metaphor

The biology isn't decoration.
It's prediction.

  • Facebook outrage loops — Not evil design. Organism optimizing for metabolic rate (engagement). The nervous system amplifies whatever feeds fastest.
  • Troll invasions — Pathogens exploiting immune gaps. Moderation is the immune response. Heavy moderation selects for more sophisticated pathogens. This is evolution.
  • TikTok's rise — A metabolic shift. An organism with a faster metabolism (shorter content, faster dopamine loops) outcompeting slower organisms for attention energy.
  • Twitter's collapse — Autoimmune disorder. The organism began attacking its own healthy cells (verified users, trusted accounts) while pathogens multiplied.
🔮
Why It Matters
Right model → visible future. Wrong model → constant surprise. The organism framework is the right model for social platforms.
⚠️
The Builder's Trap
Most founders treat platforms as distribution channels. They're building on an organism they don't understand. That's why they keep getting surprised.

Chapter 3 · The Model The Career Arc
The Barabási-Albert Model

The Influencer Economy
was inevitable. Not accidental.

Networks that grow by preferential attachment — where new nodes connect to already-connected nodes — produce scale-free networks with power-law degree distributions. A tiny number of nodes capture most of the connections.

Old Model: Broadcast
One node → many edges → many people. Star topology. TV, radio, newspapers. Central node controls the signal.
New Model: Social
Many nodes → many edges → emergent influence. Scale-free. A few hubs capture most attention via preferential attachment.
The Business Insight
The power law is physics, not policy. Design around it — not against it. Hubs, bridges, hub-services. All three are valid positions.
Chapter 3 · Venture 4 The Career Arc
Art + Equity Attribution for Creatives

ARTIQUITY — Reversing the Edges

In the creative graph, value flows downstream but attribution flows upstream poorly. Creators generate edges that carry their work — but get cut out of the value loop.

↔️
Graph Concept: Directed vs. Undirected
Most creative economies are directed graphs — value only flows one way. Artiquity reverses edges to create attribution paths. Making the graph bidirectional.
⚖️
The Mission
When value flows through an edge, the originating node gets credited. Attribution IS the edge structure of the creative economy.
Chapter 3 · Venture 5 The Career Arc
Physical Systems Airports · Real Estate · Infrastructure

ENGINUITY — The Constraint Graph

Physical infrastructure modeled as graphs. An airport: runways are edges, gates are nodes, aircraft are traversing agents, airlines are subgraphs, regulations are edge constraints.

  • New route = new edge — Does it violate adjacent constraints?
  • Storm closure = node failure — How does it cascade?
  • AI scenario planning — Traverse thousands of graph configurations to find optimal paths.
⛓️
Graph Concept: Constraint Graphs
Model physical systems as graphs with constraints on edges and nodes. Use graph algorithms to find optimal paths through scenario space. This is what AI is actually doing — graph search at scale.
🤖
Why AI Changes This
AI traverses larger graph spaces faster. It finds non-obvious paths that human planners miss. The model is still a graph — AI just searches it better.
