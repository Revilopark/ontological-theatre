Design Philosophy

The Convertible Principle

Every major Verneccia Étienne piece is conceived with multiplicity in mind. One garment — five, ten, infinite occasions. The convertible feature is not a gimmick; it is the architectural intention from the very first croquis.

Natural fabrics move, breathe, and age with grace. Merino wool. Silk organza. Taffeta. Lace. Every yard sourced from International Silks and Woollens, Beverly Hills — because quality begins before the first cut.

01

Natural Fabrics Only

Merino wool, silk organza, taffeta, lace. Never synthetic. ISW-sourced.

02

Named Pieces

Signature couture carries a name — Xanadu, Luna, AnDreeAna, Elle.

03

Convertible Design

Every major piece transforms. One garment, infinite occasions.

04

Couture Construction

Croquis to sloper to muslin. Every seam deliberate. Every fit final.

Creative Process

The Verneccia Way

"I take time to develop my croquis — 7 to 8 figures, each one proportionate and elongated. Every illustration is built on that foundation."

The creative process at VE is methodical and unhurried. From the first dream of a silhouette to the final fitting, every stage serves the garment's ultimate purpose: to move with the body that wears it.

The VE Client

  • Knows her fabrics by hand, not by label
  • Invests in pieces that outlast seasons
  • Chooses natural fibers — always
  • Values construction as much as silhouette
  • Wants one garment to do many jobs
01

Dream

The silhouette exists before the pencil touches paper. The vision comes first — always.

→
02

Croquis

Seven to eight elongated figures, each proportionate. The fashion illustration template that anchors all design work.

→
03

Illustration

The garment placed on the croquis. Colour, drape, and proportion resolved in illustration before any pattern is cut.

→
04

Flat Sketch

Technical drawing. Construction lines. Seam placements. The translation from dream to document.

→
05

Sloper

The master pattern block, built to the client's measurements. Every VE commission begins with its own sloper.

→
06

Muslin

The toile. Problems are solved here — in inexpensive fabric — before a single yard of silk organza is touched.

→
07

Self

The garment is cut in its true fabric. The designer's hand is in every seam from this point forward.

→
08

Construct

Couture construction. Hand-stitching where it counts. Boning, structure, and bead-work applied with patience.

→
09

Fit

The final fitting is a ceremony. The garment meets the body that will carry it into the world.

The Designer

Verneccia Étienne

Verneccia Étienne is a Los Angeles-based luxury fashion designer whose practice spans couture construction, convertible design, and editorial ready-to-wear. She has shown at fashion week, where her named couture pieces first introduced the world to the VE signature: a gold metal rivet monogram tag, pressed into every garment she sends into the world.

Her design philosophy begins with the fabric. Every collection is built on natural fibers — Merino wool, silk organza, taffeta, lace — sourced primarily from International Silks and Woollens in Beverly Hills. She believes synthetic fabric is a shortcut the garment will always reveal.

The convertible principle runs through her entire body of work. A cocktail dress that becomes a halter. A gown that becomes a skirt. A coat that becomes a cape. One garment, many lives — because the women who wear VE live complex, beautiful, demanding lives.
