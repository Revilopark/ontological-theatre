Regulatory Compliance

⚠️

RYZE dropped claims after NAD inquiry Sept 2025

Regulators are actively monitoring the mushroom coffee category. Be extra careful with claims.

✅ SAFE Claims (Structure/Function)

"Contains Lion's Mane mushroom, which supports nerve growth factor production"
Structure/function claim for Lion's Mane. Factual and safe.
"Broccoli microgreens contain up to 100x more sulforaphane than mature broccoli"
Nutrient content claim. Backed by Johns Hopkins research.
"Rich in antioxidants"
General nutrient content claim. Very safe.
"Supports the body's natural detoxification processes"
General wellness claim. Acceptable structure/function language.

❌ NEVER Say These

"Prevents Alzheimer's"
Disease claim. Requires FDA pre-approval.
"Fights cancer cells"
Disease claim. Will trigger FDA enforcement.
"Reduces risk of Type 2 diabetes"
Health claim. Requires substantial scientific evidence.
Any use of "cures" or "treats"
Makes product a drug under FDA definition.

📚 Three-Tier FDA Claims Framework

Health Claims

Require FDA pre-approval and substantial scientific evidence. Only ~12 exist.
Example: "Calcium reduces osteoporosis risk"

Nutrient Content Claims

Must meet specific FDA thresholds. "High in," "Good source of," etc.
Example: "High in Vitamin C" (≥20% DV)

Structure/Function Claims

No FDA pre-approval needed for food. Describe how nutrients affect body structure/function.
This is our sweet spot for functional foods
