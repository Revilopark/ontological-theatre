Section 01 — Executive Summary

An Existential Threat to RCM

The medical revenue cycle management (RCM) industry faces an existential threat from AI-powered transformation that goes far beyond "workflow automation." Using the Inkwell Trinity Graph framework, we can see that AI won't just make RCM more efficient—it will fundamentally eliminate the information asymmetries and coordination complexities that justify percentage-based pricing models.

Your $300M valuation target for a $20M profit business (15× multiple) assumes the current business model remains viable. The Trinity Graph reveals why it won't.

Section 02 — Current Value Proposition

Information Arbitrage

Traditional RCM companies like yours profit from three core asymmetries:

🧠

Knowledge Asymmetry

Healthcare providers don't understand the labyrinthine rules of payer contracts, coding, and compliance. RCM companies have accumulated institutional knowledge over decades. This expertise commands premium pricing.

Vulnerable
🔗

Coordination Complexity

Managing claims across hundreds of payers, each with unique requirements. Tracking denials, appeals, and payment patterns. Human-intensive exception handling.

Vulnerable
🔒

Opacity of Performance

Providers can't easily benchmark their own potential recovery rates. Percentage-based fees hide the actual cost-per-transaction. Limited transparency into what work is actually being done.

Vulnerable

The percentage fee model (typically 3–8% of collections) only works because these asymmetries persist.
