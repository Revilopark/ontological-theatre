Section B — Scope of Work

The Mechanical/HVAC Subcontractor shall furnish all labor, materials, equipment, tools, supervision, engineering, and services necessary to complete the following systems in strict accordance with the contract documents.

B.1 Central Chilled Water Plant — 12,000 Ton Capacity

  • Water-cooled centrifugal chillers: six (6) × 2,000-ton units with VSD (N+1 redundancy)
  • Refrigerant: R-514A or approved low-GWP alternative
  • Primary, secondary, and tertiary chilled water pumping systems
  • Cooling towers: six (6) induced-draft cells, 2,500-ton capacity each
  • Condenser water treatment: chemical feed, filtration, blowdown control
  • Thermal energy storage evaluation (2,000 ton-hour minimum if selected)
  • Full instrumentation: flow meters, BTU meters, differential pressure sensors

B.2 Process Cooling Water (PCW) System

  • Separate closed-loop system independent from comfort chilled water
  • Temperature control: ±0.5°F at point of use
  • Redundant pumping (N+1), plate-and-frame heat exchangers
  • 5-micron filtration, UV sterilization, conductivity monitoring
  • Supply temperature: 60°F ±0.5°F
  • Capacity: 1,500 GPM minimum

B.3 Cleanroom Air Handling

  • Make-up air units (MAUs): twelve (12) units, 100% outside air, 50,000 CFM each (600,000 CFM total capacity)
  • MAU features: gas-fired heat, DX pre-cool, chilled water final cool, steam humidification
  • Recirculation air handlers (RCAUs): twenty (20) units, 40,000 CFM each, ULPA filtration
  • Fan filter units (FFUs): approximately 3,000 units, 4'×4', ULPA grade, EC motor driven
  • Cleanroom pressurization cascade: fab (+0.05" w.c.) → chase → subfab → general building
  • Air change rates: 300-600 ACH in ISO 5 areas

B.4 Process Exhaust Systems (CRITICAL)

Separate, dedicated exhaust systems are required for each category. Cross-contamination between exhaust streams is strictly prohibited.

  • General Exhaust (GEX): galvanized steel ductwork, roof-mounted fans
  • Acid Exhaust (AEX): FRP ductwork, FRP fans, packed-tower scrubbers — 8 systems
  • Solvent/Organic Exhaust (SEX): welded steel duct, explosion-proof fans, thermal oxidizer treatment — 6 systems
  • Toxic/Pyrophoric Exhaust (TEX): welded SS316L duct, dedicated scrubbers or burn boxes — 4 systems
  • Heat Exhaust (HEX): steel ductwork, heat recovery where feasible
  • Lithography Exhaust (LEX): temperature-controlled, vibration-isolated
  • All exhaust to be ducted above roofline per SEMI S6 and local code

B.5 Specialty Systems

  • Humidity control: ±1% RH in critical photolithography areas, ±2% RH in general fab
  • Temperature control: ±0.1°C in lithography bays, ±0.5°C general fab
  • Vibration isolation: VC-D criteria for general fab, VC-E for advanced lithography tools
  • Cleanroom pressurization monitoring: real-time differential pressure across all zones
  • Smoke detection and emergency purge systems per FM Global and SEMI S2
  • Chemical delivery room ventilation (1 CFM/ft² minimum, emergency exhaust capability)

B.6 Building Automation / BMS

  • Full direct digital controls (DDC) — BACnet/IP backbone
  • Integration with TI's existing Honeywell Experion facility management system
  • Minimum 5,000 I/O points
  • Web-based operator interface with role-based access
  • Alarming, trending, energy monitoring, fault detection & diagnostics
  • 24/7 remote monitoring capability with 99.99% uptime SLA
  • Cybersecurity per NIST 800-82 for industrial control systems

B.7 Associated Work

  • All ductwork: galvanized steel, stainless steel, FRP as required by service
  • All piping: carbon steel, copper, FRP, PVDF, stainless as required
  • Insulation: thermal and acoustic per specification (closed-cell on all chilled services)
  • Vibration isolation: spring mounts, inertia bases, flex connectors
  • Electrical connections: from MCC/panelboard to all mechanical equipment
  • Testing, adjusting & balancing (TAB): AABC or NEBB certified
  • Commissioning and integrated systems testing (IST)
  • Cleanroom validation: particle counts, air velocity, pressurization, HEPA/ULPA integrity
  • As-built drawings (AutoCAD + Revit BIM models)
  • Operations & maintenance manuals (electronic + 3 hard copies)
  • Operator training: 40 hours minimum, video-recorded
  • Warranty: 2 years from beneficial occupancy, 5 years on compressors
