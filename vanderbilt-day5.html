The Problem

Knowledge graphs are only as valuable as their accuracy

🔴
Hallucinated Relationships
AI invented connections that don't exist. Model fills gaps with plausible-sounding but false relationships.
"Steve Jobs founded Microsoft" sounds plausible but is completely wrong.
🟡
Stale Data
True when captured, false now. Knowledge graphs decay over time if not maintained.
"Twitter is owned by Elon Musk" — was true in 2022, now it's "X Corp."
🟠
Ambiguous Entities
Is "Apple" the company or the fruit? Same name, different entities. Disambiguation is critical.
"Apple" could be Apple Inc., apple (fruit), or Apple Records.

The Rules of the Graph

Ontologies

An ontology defines what CAN exist in your graph
"A CEO must be a Person who leads an Organization. That's not just business logic — it's ontological constraint."
📋 Properties
hasName, foundedIn, employs, builds — define what relationships are possible between entity types.
⚖️ Constraints
Rules about what combinations are valid. Prevents impossible relationships from existing.
Ontology Hierarchy
Thing
├─ Organization
│ ├─ Company
│ │ └─ Startup
├─ Person
│ └─ Executive
│ └─ CEO
└─ Product
└─ Software
└─ SaaS
Inheritance: CEO inherits all properties of Person
Constraint: CEO must lead an Organization
💼 For MBA Students
"Ontologies are business taxonomies
with teeth."
💼
Strategy
Define your competitive landscape systematically. Map market categories, player relationships, and opportunity gaps.
📊
Analytics
Ensure consistent data across teams. Sales, marketing, and finance use the same entity definitions.
🤖
AI Systems
Give AI systems a shared vocabulary. Enables reasoning, reduces hallucinations, improves accuracy.
"The companies that define the ontology define the market." — Tim Berners-Lee (paraphrase)

Entity Resolution

When is Nashville = Nashville?

✅ SAME ENTITY
• Nashville, TN
• Nashville, Tennessee
• Metro Nashville-Davidson County
⚠️ SIMILAR BUT DIFFERENT
• Vanderbilt University
• Vanderbilt University Medical Center
Related but separate entities
❌ COMPLETELY DIFFERENT
• Mark Johnson (CEO of Company A)
• Mark Johnson (Professor at Vanderbilt)
Same name, different people
🔧 RESOLUTION TECHNIQUES
Fuzzy Matching
String similarity algorithms (Levenshtein, Jaccard) to match variant spellings
Embedding Similarity
Vector representations that capture semantic meaning beyond text
Canonical URIs
Unique identifiers that persist across systems (Wikidata IDs, DBpedia URIs)

Verification Framework

The VCGR Framework

Validity, Completeness, Granularity, Recency
✅
Validity
Is this triple factually correct?
Source verification: Does this fact appear in multiple reliable sources?
📊
Completeness
Are we missing important entities or relations?
Coverage analysis: What should be here but isn't?
🔬
Granularity
Are we at the right level of detail?
Zoom level: Too broad? Too specific? Just right?
⏰
Recency
When was this last verified?
TTL (time to live): How quickly does this knowledge decay?
Scale

Humans can't verify 10,000 triples manually

AUTOMATED VERIFICATION PIPELINE
1
Source Triangulation
3+ independent sources must confirm the same fact
2
Temporal Validation
Check if data is current and hasn't expired
3
Logical Consistency
Does it violate ontology rules or constraints?
4
Cross-Graph Consensus
Does Wikidata/DBpedia/Google KG agree?
5
Confidence Scoring
0.0 to 1.0 confidence score per triple
