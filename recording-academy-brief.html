Central Principle

Reciprocity as Operating System

Every legal mechanism protecting recorded artists must enforce mutual value exchange — not just prohibition. The current landscape offers artists a patchwork of incomplete protections: no federal right of publicity, a copyright regime that permits sound-alikes under §114(b), moral rights that exclude music entirely, and an AI industry training on copyrighted catalogs under contested fair use claims worth billions. The political window is open. What follows is a layered, sequenced framework organized around three program pillars: THE COVENANT, ORIGIN, and THE MESH.

IAM Analysis · Bridge Finding · Cross-Graph
Originality → Autonomy → Sovereignty
An artist's creative uniqueness is the direct, 1-hop path to their financial independence. Artists cannot exercise sovereignty over their creative identity without autonomy over how their works are used — and autonomy requires the reciprocal exchange of transparency, consent, and compensation.

The Extractive Mimicry Pattern

The IAM identifies the core problem precisely: in entertainment IP, reciprocity is a provenance and attribution problem. When AI platforms ingest copyrighted recordings, the exchange_balance_ratio collapses below 0.4 — a measurable extraction pattern. AI companies engage in surface-level reciprocity (licensing deals, consent language) while trust_accrual_rate flatlines because no genuine mutual investment flows back to creators.

Every unreciprocated use is a broken loop — a discrete trust debt that, if left unaddressed, collapses the system from genuine reciprocity into extractive mimicry.

Praxis Rune · Locked-In Realities

The Irreversible Diagnosis

The Praxis Rune was unambiguous on four realities the Recording Academy must build around, not wish away:

  1. AI-generated music is already in distribution pipelines, competing on the same algorithmic shelf as human work.
  2. Per-stream royalty rates are structurally set — they are not rising.
  3. Sync licensing is consolidating into platform-native libraries — TikTok, YouTube, and Instagram are building internal catalogs.
  4. Music is no longer scarce. Attention is. Every program, every tool, every precedent cited below operates inside these four facts.

The §114(b) Amendment — Most Impactful Single Change

Amend 17 U.S.C. §114(b) to exclude AI-generated simulations from the sound-alike permission. Add: "sounds generated by artificial intelligence systems trained on copyrighted sound recordings" are not covered by this exception. Targeted, defensible, and closes the single largest statutory gap exploited by AI voice cloners. Nearly impossible to oppose without explicitly defending AI voice theft.

Legislative Landscape

What Exists, What's Moving, What's Stalled

The NO FAKES Act (S. 1367 / H.R. 2794)

Creates federal right of publicity for voice and visual likeness. Prohibits unauthorized digital replicas. 70-year post-mortem protection. DMCA-style notice-and-takedown. Section 230 exemption for digital replicas.

Support: 12 Senate sponsors (6R, 5D): Coons, Blackburn, Klobuchar, Tillis. 400+ artist endorsements. YouTube, OpenAI, Amazon, Adobe, and Disney all support it. GovTrack: 5% chance of enactment in current form — concentrate political capital here first.

Reciprocity Gap

The NO FAKES Act is a prohibition instrument, not a value-exchange instrument. Does not address AI training, does not protect artistic style, does not create compensation mechanisms. THE COVENANT must supplement it with threshold-triggered licensing that converts the prohibition into revenue.

The TRAIN Act (S. 2455 / H.R. 7209)

Administrative subpoena process — copyright owners can compel AI developers to disclose which works were used in training. If developer fails to comply: rebuttable presumption they copied the work. Sponsors: Blackburn, Welch, Hawley, Schiff. Foundational for ORIGIN — transparency is the precondition for all enforcement.

Already Signed Into Law

  • TAKE IT DOWN Act (signed May 19, 2025): First federal law limiting harmful AI content. 48-hour removal requirement. Passed 409-2 House, unanimous Senate.
  • ELVIS Act (Tennessee, March 21, 2024): Gold-standard state law. Passed 93-0 House, 30-0 Senate. Criminal and civil liability. Targets both users AND tool providers.
  • California AB 2602 / AB 1836 (signed Sept 17, 2024): Contract protections + posthumous digital replica rights.

The 99–1 Senate Vote

July 2025: The Senate voted 99-1 to kill a proposed 10-year federal moratorium on state AI regulation. Senator Blackburn broke with her party to co-sponsor the amendment — pivotal. This demonstrates that even in a polarized Congress, artist protection commands near-unanimous support when framed correctly.

Active Litigation · 2024–2026

Cases Defining the Law in Real Time

The $1.5 Billion Precedent

Bartz v. Anthropic (N.D. Cal., June 2025): Largest copyright settlement in U.S. history — ~$3,000 per work for 500,000 books. Piracy negates fair use. Anthropic must destroy pirated training libraries. Concord Music immediately amended their Anthropic complaint to add piracy allegations. This is the hammer.

The "Sue → Settle → Partner" Pattern

UMG/Warner settlements with Udio (November 2025): "Strategic agreement" — Udio trains exclusively on licensed catalog, artists retain "full control." Financial terms confidential. This opacity IS the Reciprocity problem. THE MESH must ensure terms flow through to artists, not just labels.

Warner/Suno (November 2025): Suno will launch "licensed models" in 2026. UMG and Sony claims continue.

Active Cases with Structural Implications

  • Concord/UMG v. Anthropic: $3B+ across 20,000+ songs. January 2026 "Concord II" adds 20,517 works obtained through mass piracy.
  • RIAA v. Suno (D. Mass.): First case against AI for sound recording infringement. Suno admitted using copyrighted music; claims fair use. Up to $150,000 per work.
  • NYT v. OpenAI (S.D.N.Y.): Cross-domain precedent. Contributory infringement survives. Court compelled 20 million anonymized conversation logs. Part of MDL with 16+ lawsuits.
  • GEMA v. OpenAI (Munich, November 2025): First European court to find that BOTH training on AND outputting copyrighted lyrics constitutes infringement under German law.
  • Getty v. Stability AI (UK, November 2025): AI model weights are NOT "copies" of training images — UK-specific, but may influence U.S. arguments.
International Architecture

Global Regulatory Landscape

EU AI Act — The De Facto Global Standard

Article 53(1)(d): Mandatory training content summaries (template published July 24, 2025). Article 50: AI-generated audio must be marked in machine-readable format. Recital 106: Obligations apply extraterritoriality — regardless of where training occurs. Fines: €15 million or 3% of global annual turnover.

Any AI company serving EU markets (all of them) must comply. Effective August 2, 2025 (GPAI obligations); August 2, 2026 (transparency marking). This is the international lever.

Global Split

Creator-Protective

Australia · Brazil · South Korea

Australia rejected TDM exception outright (Oct 2025). Brazil: mandatory remuneration for AI training (passed Senate Dec 2024). South Korea KOMCA: 0% AI rule — all songs must be certified as created without AI.

Opt-Out Compromise

EU · UK

EU: opt-out model with transparency requirements. UK: abandoned broad TDM exception (2023) after creative industry backlash; now consulting on EU-style approach.

Permissive

Japan (narrowing)

Article 30-4 permits "information analysis" use — but Agency for Cultural Affairs narrowed in 2024: training specifically to replicate copyrighted expressions is NOT protected.

WIPO and the International Path

12 sessions of the WIPO Conversation on IP and Frontier Technologies since 2019. SCCR work plan (SCCR/47/7): explicitly states it is "not aimed at establishing international legislation" at this stage. WIPO's new AI Infrastructure Interchange (AIII, launched March 2026) focuses on technical standards without requiring treaty action.

Most viable path: A WIPO protocol supplementing Berne/WCT — following how WCT and WPPT (1996) addressed digital challenges without amending Berne itself. Realistic timeline: 2030+. TRIPS dispute settlement to challenge overbroad TDM exceptions is the near-term international lever.

Coalition Infrastructure

CISAC: 5 million creators across 116 organizations, led by ABBA's Björn Ulvaeus. IFPI Global Report 2025: music revenues up 5% to $29.6 billion — with AI copyright concerns flagged as structural risk. Both organizations are aligned on voluntary collective licensing as preferred mechanism.
